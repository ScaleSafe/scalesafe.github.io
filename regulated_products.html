High-Risk AI Systems under EU AI Act

Under Article 6 of the EU AI Act, AI systems intended to be used as a safety component of the following products are automatically considered high risk:

  • Machinery: Covers various mechanical devices and installations, such as industrial machines.
  • Toys: Pertains to consumer products designed for children's play.
  • Recreational Craft and Personal Watercraft: Includes boats, yachts, jet skis, and similar water vehicles.
  • Lifts and Safety Components for Lifts: Involves elevators and their critical safety parts.
  • Equipment for Potentially Explosive Atmospheres: Refers to devices used in environments where explosive conditions may exist.
  • Radio Equipment: Encompasses devices that emit or receive radio waves for communication.
  • Pressure Equipment: Covers vessels, piping, safety valves, and other components designed to hold or transport pressurized substances.
  • Cableway Installations: Includes cable cars, ski lifts, and similar transport systems used primarily in mountainous areas.
  • Personal Protective Equipment (PPE): Encompasses items like helmets, gloves, and protective clothing.
  • Appliances Burning Gaseous Fuels: Refers to gas-operated devices such as heaters and stoves.
  • Medical Devices: Includes a broad range of instruments, machines, and implants used in healthcare.
  • In Vitro Diagnostic Medical Devices: Pertains to devices used to perform tests on samples outside a living organism, such as blood tests.
  • Civil Aviation Security: Relates to the security measures and equipment used in civil aviation.
  • Two- or Three-Wheel Vehicles and Quadricycles: Covers motorcycles, scooters, and similar small motor vehicles.
  • Agricultural and Forestry Vehicles: Includes tractors, harvesters, and other heavy machinery used in farming and forestry.
  • Marine Equipment: Encompasses items used on ships, such as safety gear and navigation devices.
  • Rail System Interoperability: Relates to the compatibility of rail infrastructure, vehicles, and systems across the EU.
  • Motor Vehicles and Trailers: Covers cars, trucks, trailers, and all related components and systems.
  • General Safety of Motor Vehicles: Focuses on the overall safety requirements for motor vehicles and their occupants.
  • Aircraft and Unmanned Aerial Vehicles (UAVs): Includes manned aircraft, drones, and related parts and equipment.

For a full list and more details, see Annex II of the EU AI Act.
